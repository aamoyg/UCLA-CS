Introduction

Reliable and timely transfer of data between avionics systems is a necessity in military and civil aircraft design. a powerful motivation for development of new data transmission systems has been the prolific growth in digital computing technology providing increasing opportunities for more modular, flexible and reliable avionics systems. This has resulted in a universal move from analogue to more rugged, digital data transmission. Thus, the emergence of the Avionics Databus system. Over the last 30 years, much work has gone into exploring new techniques for the transmission of information in aircraft. One approach that has emerged as a truly internationally recognized, generally used and commercially successful standard in the avionics industry is MIL-STD-1553.

MIL-STD-1553

MIL-STD-1553 originated around 1972 and is published by the SAE, and was specifically developed for military avionics applications. It is a very successful standard, now used on nearly all new and upgrade programs in space, fixed- and rotary-wing military applications.

1553 operates bidirectionally on a multisource, multisink basis and each unit on the bus can communicate with all the others. Up to 32 users (terminals) can be connected to the bus with data transmitted serially at 1Mbps in words of 20 bits. Normal system design involves dual redundancy, whereby the complete cable and coupling assembly is duplicated such that if a failure appears in the primary assembly, the secondary can be used.

Transmission on the bus is managed through a 'Command--Response' protocol whereby all transactions are authorized exlusively by a master, termed the bus controller (BC). Other terminals are called remote terminasl (RTs) of which there can be between 1 and 31 (each with 31 subaddresses) or monitors (Ms). Various message formats are supported (BC to RT, RT to BC, and RT to RT, along with bradcast-type transmissions where one user can communicate with ohter users). In the vast majority of applications, connections to the bus are achieved through transformer couplers thereby providing good electrical isolation between transmit and receive circuitry.
